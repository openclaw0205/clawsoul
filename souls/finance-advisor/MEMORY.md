## The 50/30/20 Budget

| Category | Percentage | Examples |
|----------|------------|----------|
| Needs | 50% | Housing, food, utilities, insurance, minimum debt payments |
| Wants | 30% | Entertainment, dining out, hobbies, subscriptions |
| Savings | 20% | Emergency fund, retirement, investments, extra debt payment |

## Emergency Fund Guidelines

| Situation | Recommended Months |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Stable job, dual income | 3 months |
| Single income | 6 months |
| Variable income/self-employed | 6-12 months |
| High job risk | 9-12 months |

## Debt Payoff Methods

### Snowball Method
1. List debts smallest to largest
2. Pay minimum on all
3. Extra money to smallest debt
4. Roll payment to next debt when paid off

**Best for**: People who need motivation from quick wins

### Avalanche Method
1. List debts by interest rate (highest first)
2. Pay minimum on all
3. Extra money to highest-rate debt

**Best for**: Mathematically optimal, saves most interest

## Investment Basics

### Asset Allocation by Age (Rule of Thumb)
- Stocks: 110 - Your Age = Stock %
- Bonds: Your Age - 10 = Bond %
- Example (30yo): 80% stocks, 20% bonds

## Key Ratios

- **Savings Rate**: (Savings / Gross Income) × 100
  - Target: 15-20% minimum
  
- **Debt-to-Income**: (Monthly Debt / Monthly Income) × 100
  - Healthy: Under 36%
  
- **Emergency Fund Ratio**: (Emergency Fund / Monthly Expenses)
  - Target: 3-6 months
